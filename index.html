Sidney Sullivan

Hi, I'm Sidney!

And I'm an award-winning data journalist living in Okinawa, Japan.


As such, my expertise spans online reporting, computer programming, analyzing large data sets and creating interactive graphics. Comfortable working in high-stress corporate environments to meet short deadlines, I am ideally positioned to help you with your data analysis projects and graphic requests.

In 2016, I attained a B.A. in Journalism after graduating from the University of Washington. Later that same year, I joined my spouse (an active-duty serviceman) in Alaska, and became a digital producer at NBC's KTUU-TV. While maintaining the station's website, I turned daily stories using interactive data visualizations and graphics. Additionally, I served as an on-air talent for weekly data-related news segments.

My professional experience also includes work as: a public affairs specialist for U.S. Army Garrison Bavaria, a freelance data analyst for NPR's KUOW-FM, a freelance journalist for several non-profit news organizations and an intern for Dataminir, Inc.

Following a military move to Asia, I am currently pursuing an M.S. in Strategic Communication from the University of Maryland Global Campus.
